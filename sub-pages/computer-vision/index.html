Computer Vision

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand the visual world, trying to mimic the human brain's cabalility for recognising certain patterns and visual information.

Home

Recent developments have lead to computer vision being used during surgery to reduce surgical complications. In this specific case, a software is used to identify areas of organs that are safe to dissect. The prototype was developed by feeding hudreds of hours of hours of gallbladder surgeries and integrating annotations from expert surgeons. The model trained has an accuracy between 93-95%.

One of the biggest growing areas for computer vision is in autonomous vehicles, in which artificial intelligence can categorise and interpret a variety of things that is visible to it. This includes street signs, road markings, pedestrians, etc. Closely related to this, computer vision and machine learning can also be used to understand the driver status, based on analysis of the head pose, eye tracking and upper body movement.
